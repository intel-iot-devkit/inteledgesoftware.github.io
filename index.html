Artificial Intelligence (AI) has unleashed a new era of creativity and ingenuity. AI is helping us solve highly complex real-world challenges across industry verticals. At Intel, we are on a mission to open AI and data science to more use cases, in more places, for more innovators and software developers. We are working closely with the AI community to accelerate discoveries and make meaningful progress in how we use AI to add value to our work and lives.

Intel offers a range of resources to help application developers get the most out of their AI deployment:

  • The Intel® Distribution of OpenVINO™ toolkit is an open-source toolkit that enables you to deploy high-performance deep learning inference and streamline development. Since the toolkit is compatible with the most popular frameworks, there is minimal disruption and maximum performance.
  • The Intel® DevCloud provides instant access to the latest edge hardware as well as Jupyter* Notebook-based tutorials, sample applications, and reference implementations. Your AI workloads can be built, tested, and run for free on Intel® hardware.

This curated list of Intel repositories on GitHub will serve as a starting point to help you leverage our AI tools and developer resources to their full potential and stay up to date on the latest methods. Explore more now!

Optimize Your Model for High-Performance Inference

To achieve the best performance, the model must be optimized for a given hardware. Successfully deploy deep neural networks by providing adaptations for the hardware and computational methods.

Neural Network Compression Framework (NNCF)

Take advantage of a suite of advanced algorithms that are designed to work with models from PyTorch and TensorFlow, and bring additional performance enhancements.

OpenVINO™ Training Extensions (OTE)

Train deep learning models and convert them using a spectrum of best-in-class algorithms for optimized inference with preserved accuracy.

Post-Training Optimization Tool

Post-training Optimization Tool (POT) is designed to accelerate the inference of deep learning models by applying special methods without model retraining or fine-tuning, for example, post-training 8-bit quantization.
